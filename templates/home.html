Introduction

This is a fan-made site dedicated to documenting the complete musical works of Masaya Oya. Going by the name かめりあ (Romanized as Camellia), he has produced hundreds of original songs and contributed music to various rhythm games such as beatmania IIDX, SOUND VOLTEX, Dance Dance Revolution, Arcaea, Beat Saber, and dozens more. On his own music label, かめるかめりあ, he has released over twenty solo albums of original music, 4 solo albums through Exit Tunes and Pony Canyon featuring music from his contributions to various Konami-produced arcade games, and 5 collaboration albums with singer Nanahira. In his earlier years he notably produced large amounts of Vocaloid music, almost all of which was performed by 初音ミク (Hatsune Miku), as well as arranging dozens of songs from the Touhou game series on labels such as Alstromeria Records. Throughout the years he has contributed music to several doujin music circles' albums and been a member of a few musical groups, notably Quarks with singer kradness, and Kasai Harcores with fellow producers DJ Genki and C-show. More recently, he has become a popular composer in the growing V-Tuber scene, producing the Hololive English Myth Image Soundtrack, as well as several original songs for talents from major V-Tuber companies such as Hololive and Nijisanji.

Notes

- If you're looking for the track 築地で一番美味いもの, I have chosen not to include it as I believe that KASAI HARCORES (and therefore Camellia) did not actually play a role in the making of this track. On the official page for KASAI HARCORES EP the track is listed as 築地で一番美味いもの feat. 築地ハードスタイラーズ, which would imply that, like the other tracks that feature Nanahira, yukacco, and Edoist, the song was made by both KASAI HARCORES and 築地ハードスタイラーズ (Tsukiji Hardstylers), and as a result this is what many discography sites list. However on all major streaming services and DJ Genki's bandcamp the track is solely attributed Tsukiji Hardstylers, unlike the other tracks where both KASAI and the featured artist(s) are credited. It's also worth mentioning that on the official page each track is not listed next to a track number, but rather an inning-number. With the album being baseball themed this makes sense, but if you know anything about baseball than you know an inning consists of two parts, the top of the inning in which the home team is fielding, and the bottom of the inning, in which the away team is fielding. And for every track in the tracklist, in red, the inning number is followed by the kanji 回表 meaning the top of the inning, all except for 築地で一番美味いもの where, in blue, the inning number is followed by the kanji 回裏 meaning the bottom of the inning. In this analogy I believe that the difference in color and inning half is meant to imply that another team (that being Tsukiji Hardsytlers) is now fielding (making the song). You can click here for the album's page to see what I mean.


- If you are looking for the song 心ふたつ, I am fairly confident that vgmdb is wrong and this is not actually Camellia (wouldn't be the first time it was wrong). I believe this simply because for how recent of a song it is, the fact that camellia has never expressed that he worked on it is strange. In addition, Camellia NEVER writes his name in katakana (like カメリア). The only time I have ever seen it written this way was from poor translation, and yet every official source and distributor of PHANTASY STAR ONLINE 2 キャラクターソングCD～Song Festival～VI, writes it as カメリア, which leads me to believe it is indeed someone else.


- Regarding the songs Electioneering and "8'14", both of which are credited to Camellia on discogs, I have strong reason to believe that these are incorrect credits. I am fairly confident that both of them (if not, at least "8'14") are songs by a Japanese indie rock group that also goes by the name Camellia. The only information I could find regarding them was this page, but because they are so obscure I can not draw a direct link between them and the songs. The best link I have found is this video of "camellia" playing "8'14". On the same channel, under this video of "camellia" playing "untitled", I found the link to the band's page. As for Electioneering, it is so out of character and out of style for Camellia, and so in-character and in-style for "camellia" that I can only assume that this is the same band, but I have not found any connections between the two besides musical style and similar timings. Also, Camellia has never made any public acknoloedgements of either of these songs, not on his website (new and old), not on twitter, not on anything.


- I have seen a theory circling that 上ノ瀬つかさ (Tsukasa Kaminose) is actually Camellia under an alias, and even vgmdb recognizes it as such, however from what I've seen this theory is pretty baseless. As a result you will not find any works by 上ノ瀬つかさ here.
